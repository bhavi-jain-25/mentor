JEE Exam Information

What is Jee?

JEE, or Joint Entrance Examination, is an all-India common entrance examination conducted for admission to various undergraduate engineering programs across India. It is comprised of two parts: JEE Main and JEE Advanced.

Important Dates

  • Registration Start Date: 5 September
  • Registration End Date: 30 September
  • JEE Main Exam Date: Usually in April, May
  • JEE Advanced Exam Date: Usually in May
  • Result Declaration: Usually in June

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the JEE exam, candidates must:

  • Have passed 10+2 or equivalent examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • Have scored a minimum aggregate of 75% in the qualifying examination (65% for SC/ST candidates).
  • Have appeared or be appearing in the respective qualifying examination for the current academic year.

Exam Pattern

The JEE exam consists of two papers:

  1. JEE Main: This paper consists of multiple-choice questions from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  2. JEE Advanced: This paper consists of multiple-choice questions, numerical answer type questions, and comprehension-based questions.

Cutoff Marks

The cutoff marks for JEE vary each year and are determined by factors such as the difficulty level of the exam, the number of candidates appearing, and the availability of seats. Generally, candidates need to score above a certain percentile to qualify for admission to top engineering colleges.

Total Number of Students

On average, over 1.5 million students appear for the JEE Main exam annually. The number of students appearing for JEE Advanced is significantly lower, usually around 200,000.

Girl Ratio

While the number of girls appearing for the JEE exam has been increasing steadily over the years, they still represent a minority of the total candidates. The girl ratio is typically around 25-30% of the total number of candidates.

Perks for girls in jee

  • Scholarship Opportunities: Several scholarships and financial assistance programs exist to support students pursuing higher education in engineering, including girls. These scholarships often consider factors such as academic merit, financial need, and underrepresented groups in STEM fields.
  • Reservation Quotas: Some educational institutions and state governments have reservation quotas or special provisions for girls in engineering programs, ensuring a certain percentage of seats are allocated to female candidates.
  • Government Initiatives: Governments at the national and state levels often launch initiatives to promote girls' education and participation in STEM fields. These initiatives may include financial support, infrastructure development, and policy interventions to address gender disparities in education.
